FPS

-- First Person Shooter

Arguably the most well known genre in this generation of gamers. First person shooter games as the name suggests allows the player to control their character and eliminate enemy targets from a first person point of view. The original purpose of FPS games were made not for entertainment but for computerized military simulations. As computer processors and game engines become more powerful, FPS games begins to gain popularity among the gamers and many new subgenere of FPS games begins to appear. On paper, FPS games are easy to play, just point your mouse and click to shoot your gun; but there could be other elements of the game that demends the player's ability to control the recoil of their gun, to avoid map hazards, to use a variety of tools and abilities to give themselves a tactical advantage and so on. The guides presented below will help you adapt to the FPS genre in general.

Controller and Mouse

Mouse and controllers are the most common type of user input for FPS games. The main difference between a controller and a mouse is that the mosue uses laser or optical tracking to operate and the controller uses buttons and joysticks to operate. The controller settings are usually more simple and you could just refer to the user manual that comes out of the box with your console. The mouse however usually requires more tuning.

Adjusting Sensitivity

In the settings menu of most games, the option to change the sensivity of the mouse or controller will be avaliable. The recommended sensivity will be the one the user is most comfortable with. Players should play around with the settings in practice or testing lobbies in game to find the best sensivity for them. Most mouse users are comfortable with the sensitivity that allows them to turn their character 360 degrees when they drag their mouse across their mousepad, but this is not always the best setting.
