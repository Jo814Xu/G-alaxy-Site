Indie Games

      An independent video game, or an indie game, is a video game that is often created without the financial support of a publisher, although some games funded by a publisher are still considered "indie". Indie games often focus on innovation and rely on digital Indie gaming saw a rise in mainstream popularity in the latter half of the 2000s, primarily due to new online distribution methods and development tools. Some indie games have been very successful financially, such as Undertale, Braid, World of Goo, and Minecraft.

      Independent game developers, team members are generally less than 10 people, the most common is a small team of only one or two people, the workplace is very casual. Unlike commercial games, developing indie games doesn't necessarily give developers revenue. Only a few independent games can be sold using physical channels, and most rely on lower cost digital download modes.

Journey

      During the Journey playing, the player will control a character in a robe and walk to the top of a distant mountain in the vast desert. On the way, the player can randomly meet another player who has the same journey; although the two can help each other on the road, they can't communicate by voice or text like other games, and they can't see each other's names. The only way to communicate between players is to sing without words. This humming will have an impact on the game world, allowing players to continue the game through the level. The development team is forced to pass the game, let the player feel the smallness and loneliness in the world, and then become a companion with the stranger, so that the player really has an interest to socialize with other players and establish an emotional bond.

      The character controlled by the player is wrapped in a deep red robes, wearing a black mask and only seeing the eyes. There is a long scarf behind the head of the character. After touching the checkmark in the level, the player's scarf will lengthen, and the runes on the scarf will increase to extend the time and distance of the player's character floating and jumping. Floating needs to consume the luminous runes representing the energy on the scarf, after which the rune light will slowly recover automatically, or the companion can sing the notes to quickly recover, or in the level to contact the fluttering pieces of the cloth to recover. The game scene is also dotted with larger, worn-out strips that players can sing around and turn them into deep red print strips similar to the robes worn by the characters. This is also one of the game's enchanting elements. Transforming these big cloths can have different effects on the game world, such as releasing pieces of cloth, forming bridges, or floating the player. Players also need to use this door to continue the journey. On the way, players will also encounter cloth flying creatures like kites, some of which will lead the players or accompany the players. At the end of the game, there will be hostile rock monsters. If the characters accidentally touch them, the scarf will be pulled off and the player's ability will be reduced.
